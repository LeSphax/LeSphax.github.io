Perspective Shift


Technologies : Unity with C#

This is a puzzle game that tries to introduce new mechanics to the player with very little explanations. At the moment there are only puzzles and no real structure to navigate them.

Implementing the undo feature while having animations between the different board states was quite challenging and I had to build a complex hierarchy of objects and events. Another challenge was to switch between the 2D and 3D modes.

It is also my first real try at level design.

SlideBall


This game is multiplayer, it is better to play with friends but if you are alone you can play it on two windows (just opening two tabs won't work) to have both client and host perspective. If you want to play with me I am also up ready to play a game :)


Technologies : Unity with C#

Tools : GIMP, Blender, Maya, Audacity

I really liked the mod Hover on Starcraft II and I am trying to create a similar game.
For the networking side, I started to use Photon Unity Network but I then switched to WebRTC to get a better latency. This means I had to recreate several functionnalities like RPCs and distributing packets to the different entities and I am hosting my own signaling server that handles the rooms creation. I also tried several methods to synchronise the player avatars and the ball accross the network, like client side prediction, extrapolation, or interpolation.

This is the game I am currently working on (May 2017). The technical side starts to work reliably well when playing with 8 people so I am starting to experiment more with the gameplay.

FlyHigher


Technologies : Unity with C#, XML
Tools : GIMP

Working with 3 other students, I made a game to teach aeronautics to children from 6 to 10 years old and produced a small paper containing recommandation for creating educational entertainment.

After learning how to use Unity, I created two small games, the save management and translation modules and half of the game structure and UI.

Designing, developing and testing the game took about 100 hours of work from each of us. I also organised and conducted user tests with primary school children.

Waves


Technologies : Unity with C#

In this game, you follow a signal across several beautiful environments. The signal fades with time and you have to gather other waves to keep it strong. You can do this by changing the signal's amplitude and frequency.

I worked on this game with 4 artists, 1 sound designer and 2 developpers. I programmed everything related to the signal, its controls, the way it looks and how it changes the sound. I also programmed the waves that you collect and the "friend" waves that join you when the universe changes.
